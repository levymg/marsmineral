From Waste to Profits

Pelletizing your Bio-Mass residue is an easy, 5-step process that can convert it into valuable soil amendment pellets.

  1. The Pin Mixer receives ash from the storage bin and feeder, mixes it with any micro-nutrients, pelletizes the mixture and sends it to the Disc Pelletizer for further processing.

  2. The Disc Pelletizer finishes the process and produces non-dusty, free-flowing pellets, ready for drying.

  3. The dryer dries and cools the pellets, putting them onto a conveyor belt that delivers them to the screen. The dryer fuel can be bio-mass, natural gas, propane, fuel oil or electricity.

  4. The screen removes the oversized & undersized pellets sending the “good” pellets to the storage bin, located outside of the building. The storage bin can feed bulk pellets or return the pellets to the bagging line.

  5. The bagger receives pellets from the storage bin and packages the pellets automatically into 40 pound bags for use by home gardeners, nurseries and growers.

Get the Most Out of Your Bio-Mass Residue

Working with Bio-Mass

If you’re burning bio-mass fuel, you’re creating an ash residue that may be used as a fertilizer because it contains certain properties which are beneficial to plant growth.

Even if the ash lacks certain qualities, it can be upgraded by adding various nutrients or N-P-K components. Converting bio-mass into pellets puts the ash residue into a form that’s user-friendly in various land application devices, including spreaders and broadcasters.

Low dusting, free-flowing pellets/granules and custom blending are all advantages of pelletizing your bio-mass residue.

Who Can Profit From Bio-mass Residue?

  • Companies currently disposing of residue ash
  • Citizens, by adding a fortified product to their lawns & gardens
